Training

The Witbank Skydiving Club offers two methods of getting into the air for the first time:

  • Static Line Instruction
  • Accelerated Free Fall Instruction
  • Tandem Skydiving

See the equipment section on this page for an overview of the student equipment used by the club.

Static Line

Static Line instruction is the longest established method of skydiving instruction and still the most popular at Witbank. Before making a static line jump all students are given an instructional course covering all aspects of their first jump, this course generally takes a full day. Once all instruction is complete the students are ready for their first static line jump - in this form of jump the students parachute is attached to the plane via a static line. When the students exits the plane the static line pulls the parachute out of the container and deploys it - there is no need for the student to pull a rip cord. After doing three "no-load" static line jumps, where the student does nothing more than exit the aircraft, the student is placed on to dummy rip-cord pulls. On a dummy rip-cord jump the student is still attached to the plane via the static line but they also have a dummy rip-cord with which they must demonstrate their ability to succesfully pull a rip-cord and deploy their parachute for themselves. After five consecutive succesful dummy rip-cord pulls the student is cleared for freefall and the fun really begins!

A static line course is held every Saturday morning. Just arrive at the DZ by 8:30.

Tandem

If you are not sure if skydiving is for you and want to try it out before commiting yourself to a full course, or just want to experience freefall once, then tandem is for you. In a tandem jump you are attached to a tandem instructor via a special two person harness which is connected to an extra large parachute especially designed to carry two people. Tandem jumps require only a short (45 minute) instruction course and then you are straight in to freefall from 9,500 feet. The club also has available video camermen who can accompany you on your ride and film the whole lot, facial expressions and all.

Tandem skydives require booking, please call the DZ or send us an e-mail.

Equipment

Student rigs are either PISA Naro, Vulcan or Invader harnesses with PISA  Skymaster 265 main canopies and PISA Tempo Reserves.  All student rigs are equiped with Reserve Static Lines, all student freefall rigs are equiped with Cypress AAD's
