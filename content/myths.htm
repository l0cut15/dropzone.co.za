Skydiving Myths

You Can Talk In Freefall

Not unless you have one heck of a loud voice. In freefall you are moving at about 220Km/h and the wind noise is incredibly loud. If you think you can do it try doing 220Km/h in a car sticking your head out the window and talking to someone (just dont tell the cops where you got this idea) 

Everyone Plows Into the Ground Like a Sack of Potatoes

These days everyone jumps ram air canopies that are far more like an aircraft wing than the old round canopies you've seen in dozens on world war II movies. These canopies allow everyone (even first time jumpers) to have gentle tip toe landings - not the crash, thud, roll, ouch landings suffered by paratroopers. 

You Can Throw Your Parachute Out and Catch it In Freefall
 
Arnie and the like might do this in the movies but in reality we like to make sure everyone has their parachute strapped on securely BEFORE exiting the plane. Things move really fast in freefall (like about 220Km/h) and catching your parachute, let alone struggling into the harness, in 40 seconds or so is really unlikely. 

After One Jump You'll Be Skysurfing Like a Pro

Sorry, Skydiving is far from an easy sport, skysurfing looks easy on TV because the people you are watching are experts with thousands of jumps. In reality you probably wont even strap on a board until you have atleast 100 jumps. 

You Freefall For Five Minutes

Chalk another one up to the movies. In real life most skydivers jump from around 10,000 feet. This gives us about 35's of freefall, to get the five minutes or so you see in the movies we'd need to go to about 60,000 feet which requires lots of warm clothes and oxygen plus a really looooong time in the plane. Since skydivers are so lazy and hate being in the plane so much we stick to 10,000 feet. 

Everyone Who Skydives is Insane

Despite the common public perception that anyone who jumps out of a perfectly good aeroplane must be certifiably insane most of us are in fact boringly normal.
